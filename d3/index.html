This interactive network presents a comprehensive bioinformatics analysis aimed at uncovering novel regulatory mechanisms mediated by long non-coding RNAs (lncRNAs) in colorectal cancer (CRC). The study integrates multi-omic data from The Cancer Genome Atlas Program (TCGA) together with a unique local dataset from the Aosta Valley (Italy). The analysis was developed with two main goals: first, to identify lncRNAs located within genomic regions showing recurrent copy number alterations (CNAs), such as duplications and deletions, as well as regions with high mutation frequency in CRC patients; and second, to assign functional relevance to these lncRNAs by correlating their expression with that of their molecular targets, including protein-coding genes, microRNAs (miRNAs), and other lncRNAs, thereby reconstructing biologically meaningful regulatory pathways.

How to Explore the Network

  • Hover over a node to strongly highlight its direct interactions.
  • Use the mouse scroll wheel to zoom in and out of the network.
  • Click on a node to display detailed interaction information.
  • Click on gene names in the table below to access the corresponding gene entry in GeneCards.
